Before You Open the Tool

ChopRead is a decision filter, not a signal generator. Its job is to keep you out of bad trades. Most days, the right answer is to not trade. Follow this SOP every session — no exceptions.

The One Rule That Overrides Everything
If SPY is in a BEAR trend (below its 20-day SMA), do not take any CALL positions. The backtest showed a 11.2% win rate on calls during bear market phases. In a bear market: use the 07 PUTS tab for swing puts, and the 06 0DTE tab (PUT only filter) for same-day plays.

Step 02
Run the Scanner — Find Candidates

Click Scan on the Scanner tab (or use the automated Morning Scanner which runs 100+ liquid tickers in one shot). Wait for all rows to load (5–30 seconds depending on watchlist size).

The threshold is Score ≥ 65. Anything below is statistically in the dead zone — do not analyze it. The backtest showed 0% win rate at scores 68–75 in many conditions; below 65 is even worse.

Use the ⚡ PRIME ONLY toggle to filter the Morning Scanner results to only show score 65–66 setups with HV Rank ≥ 40% — the statistically cleanest window from the 18-month backtest.

Also check the scanner columns to understand WHY a ticker scored high:

  • RSI — for calls, want RSI < 50 (room to run). RSI > 65 = overbought, less ideal
  • ADX — trend strength. ≥ 25 = trending, ≥ 40 = strong trend. ADX < 20 = choppy = skip
  • HV Rank — 40–74% is the sweet spot. <40% = stock not moving enough; skip (except NVDA)
  • Vol — relative to average. 1.5×+ confirms institutions are moving the stock
  • Trend / Age — Bull or Bear plus days since EMA crossover. >20 days old = late entry risk
  • ✓0DTE badge — appears next to score when a live SPY OR signal matches the stock's direction. Highest-conviction setup. ⊘0DTE = opposite direction from SPY signal — proceed with extra caution.

Kelly Regime Badge

The Trade Rec box shows a regime badge based on your last closed trade:

  • 🔥 HOT — Last trade was a winner within 10 days. Historical WR after a win: 72.6%, PF 8.2. Size up to Half-Kelly in the Selector.
  • ❄ COLD — Last trade was a loser within 10 days. Historical WR after a loss: 7.1%, PF 0.22. Minimum size or skip.
  • (no badge) — Neutral. First trade, or last close was >10 days ago. Use standard risk %.

The 5-Point Pre-Trade Checklist

The Analyze tab shows a checklist that auto-computes. Need 4 or 5 out of 5 for high confidence. 3/5 = marginal (half size or skip). Below 3 = do not trade.

  • Macro aligned — SPY in bull trend for calls, bear for puts
  • HV Rank ≥ 40 — stock is moving enough to hit 2×ATR target
  • Score not in dead zone — score outside 68–75 range where backtest shows 0% WR
  • Cooldown clear — no losing trade on this ticker within the cooldown period
  • No earnings < 14 days — earnings cause IV spikes then crush, killing direction plays
The Cooldown Rule Explained
When you click "Send to Selector," the tool stores your entry price. If the stock is down (for a call) or up (for a put) at the next signal, the prior trade is classified as a loss. The backtest showed: after a losing TSLA call, the next signal had only a 4% win rate. The cooldown enforces a 7-day pause. After a win, it shortens to 3 days (next signal has 81% WR when prior trade was winning).
Step 04
Use the Selector — Find Your Strike

Click "Send to Selector" (only active when checklist ≥ 3/5). The Selector auto-fills ticker, price, direction, IV, and ATR. If you type a ticker directly in the Selector, clicking Find Options will automatically fetch the live price from Tradier before calculating — you never need to enter the price manually.

The Kelly Sizing Panel appears below the strike matrix after running Find Options. It shows your current regime (HOT/COLD/NEUTRAL), Full Kelly %, Half-Kelly %, and the recommended dollar amount and contract count at each level. Only size up to Half-Kelly — Full Kelly is shown for reference only.

Breakeven Check

Below the matrix, the tool shows: "Break-even: needs +X% move by expiry → within 1×ATR ✓ reachable"

  • Within 1×ATR — reachable in a normal day. Best scenario.
  • 1–1.5×ATR — possible, needs a good move. Acceptable.
  • > 2×ATR — mathematically difficult. Consider going closer to ATM.
Position Sizing — Non-Negotiable
Your "Max per trade" setting defines your maximum dollar risk. ChopRead shows Max Lots based on this. Never exceed it. If Max Lots shows <1, your account is too small for that contract — either: (a) adjust your max risk % up slightly, (b) move to a closer-to-ATM strike with a lower premium, or (c) skip the trade. Losing 2× your max risk on one trade because you bought 2 contracts instead of 1 is the fastest path to blowing an account.
Step 05
Execute at Your Broker

Before placing the order, do a 90-second live verification at your broker:

  1. Open the specific expiration in your broker's chain. Find the strike ChopRead recommended (★).
  2. Calculate bid/ask spread: (Ask − Bid) ÷ Mid × 100. If > 10%, move one strike closer to ATM and recheck. If > 20% at ATM, the contract is illiquid — skip today.
  3. Place a limit order at the mid-price.
  4. If unfilled after 60–90 seconds, raise limit by $0.02–0.05. Repeat once. If still unfilled, cancel — the market moved.
  5. Never use a market order on options.
Step 06
Log the Trade Immediately

Go to the Journal tab and log before you do anything else. Record:

  • Ticker, direction, strike, DTE at entry, contracts
  • Entry price ($/share), setup score, your reason

Set a reminder (phone alarm, calendar) for your exit check date: 14 DTE from entry, or when either stop/target is hit.

Step 07
Manage the Trade — Exit Rules

Check the trade once per day, end of day. Do not intraday-manage directional options — theta and IV noise create false signals intraday.

ConditionAction
Option loses 50% of entry valueCLOSE immediately. No exceptions. No "hoping it comes back." This is the hard stop.
Stock closes below your stop level (1.5×ATR from entry)Close — technical invalidation. The setup no longer exists.
Option gains 100% (2× entry price)Close at least 50%. Let the other 50% run with stop moved to break-even.
5 DTE remaining with open positionClose everything. Theta decay becomes brutal in the final week.
14 DTE from entryIf neither stop nor target hit — evaluate. Close if thesis changed, or hold if trend still intact with DTE > 7.

After closing: update the Journal tab with exit price and outcome.

The March–April / July–August Problem
Four months in the backtest produced near-zero win rates: March 2024 (rate panic), April 2024 (Middle East tensions), July 2024 (post-earnings rotation), August 2024 (Japan yen carry trade unwind, VIX spiked to 65). These are macro regime changes — the kind of move where every indicator looks bullish right up until the market collapses 10%+ in days.

The tool's macro filter (SPY BEAR = no calls) catches some of these — but not all, especially at the start of a regime shift. During these periods: reduce size by 50%, tighten stops to 35% instead of 50%, and when in doubt, sit out.
When Signals Are Most Likely to Fire
Signals cluster on days after a 1–3% single-day pullback in a bull trend — the RSI cools from overbought, HV Rank can spike if the move was sharp, and the EMA crossover signal sometimes refreshes. Monday/Tuesday after a Friday pullback is a historically productive signal window. The best signal days are NOT the days the stock is running — they are the days after a brief dip in an ongoing uptrend.

Quick Reference — Trade Decision Tree

Swing scanner (14-day trades):

  1. VIX check: > 30? Use spreads only. > 40? Paper trade only.
  2. SPY check: BULL → CALL setups. BEAR → switch to 07 PUTS or 0DTE PUT only.
  3. Scanner: Any ticker ≥ 65? If no → no trade today. If yes → Analyze.
  4. ✓0DTE badge: Present? Highest conviction — stock + market direction aligned.
  5. Analyze checklist: 4–5/5 passes? Trade. 3/5? Half size. < 3? Skip.
  6. HV Rank: < 40 (not NVDA)? Hard skip — stock not moving enough.
  7. Trend Age: > 20 days since crossover? Wait for a fresh setup.
  8. Selector ★ row: Break-even < 1.5×ATR? Proceed. > 2×ATR? Move strike.
  9. Bid/Ask spread: > 10%? Move closer to ATM. > 20%? Skip.
  10. Execute: Limit at mid-price. 1–2 contracts max per risk settings.
  11. Exit rules: −50% = close. +100% = close half. 5 DTE = close all.

0DTE ORB (same-day trades, VIX 18–35):

  1. Pre-market: Set direction filter (PUT only if SPY BEAR, CALL only if SPY BULL).
  2. 9:45 AM: Check signal card. GO → enter within 15 min. NO-GO → skip the day.
  3. Entry: Limit order at Δ0.35 strike shown in trade card. Verify at broker.
  4. Exit: T1 1.5× / stop −30% / 60-min time stop / 2:30 PM hard close — whichever comes first.
  5. Log it: Click + Log Trade in Paper Trade Log immediately after entry.

0DTE System
0DTE ORB Workflow (06 0DTE Tab)

This is a parallel system to the swing scanner — trade it independently. Use when VIX is 18–35 and you want a same-day directional play on SPY, QQQ, or IWM.

0DTE Step 01
Read the Pre-Market Brief & Set Direction Filter (Before 9:30 AM)

Open the 06 0DTE tab before the market opens. The pre-market brief panel auto-shows (disappears at 9:30 AM). It shows ES=F and NQ=F futures direction, VIX level, implied market move for the session, and any Signal Intel from overnight. Use this to decide your direction bias before the OR forms.

0DTE Step 02
Watch the Signal Card (9:45 AM – 11:00 AM ET)

After 9:45 AM the OR is locked. Check the key levels bar (Prev Close, Open, Day H/L) for context — is price above yesterday's close? Holding above the open? This anchors your read before looking at the signal.

The symbol cards show live status:

When a card shows ● GO, tap it to make it active. The trade card below shows: Strike (Δ0.35), Entry premium, Delta, Theta/hr, OR position bar, Stop/T1/T2 exit cards with $ P&L per contract, and exit mode (RUNNER / NORMAL / SCALP). Check the OR countdown in the header — trades after 11 AM use NORMAL or SCALP mode, not RUNNER.

0DTE Step 03
Execute — Entry Rules
  • Enter at or near the estimated entry premium shown in the trade card. Verify at your broker.
  • Use a limit order only — 0DTE spreads can be wide at fast-moving moments.
  • RUNNER mode (before 11 AM + VIX≥22): sell 50% at 2× premium, trail 50% to 2.5×
  • NORMAL mode (before 1:15 PM): sell at 1.5× premium
  • SCALP mode (after 1:15 PM): sell 100% at 1.5×, no trailing — theta is too expensive to hold
  • Check Theta/hr in the trade card. If theta exceeds $15/hr (red), your window to profit is short — enter only on clear momentum, not marginal breakouts.

Immediately after filling: tap 📡 Track Live in the trade card. This one tap pre-fills the Active Trade Tracker with the symbol, direction, strike, and your fill price — no manual typing. Enter actual contracts in the tracker, then click ➕ Add Trade.

Tap 🔔 Enable Alerts in the tracker header if you haven't already. This arms browser notifications + ntfy for automatic stop/target alerts — you won't need to watch the screen.

0DTE Step 05
Log the Trade (Build Forward Data)

There are two logging paths — use both:

  • 📡 Track Live (in the trade card) → Active Trade Tracker. Use this for real-time P&L monitoring during the trade. When the trade closes, remove it from the tracker via the ✕ button.
  • 📝 Backtest Log (in the trade card) → Paper Trade Log. Saves the signal for statistical analysis. After the trade, find it in the log and enter the exit premium + hold time to complete the record.

The Paper Trade Log builds running stats: WR, PF, total forward P&L per contract. After 20+ entries this becomes your actual edge measurement — more reliable than the 4-trade backtest sample.
